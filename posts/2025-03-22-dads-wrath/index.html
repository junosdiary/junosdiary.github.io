Gradma and Dad's Wrath

March 22, 2025 · Juno

As usual, Grandma wanted a video call with me. I haven’t seen Grandma for a while, so I just sat there and staring. Grandma tried so hard asking me to “stand up”, “walk around”, “say something”…etc. and that’s when Dad blew up, he accused Grandma always being manipulative and trying to control his life. Dad was furious, he threw a lot of f* words around, blamed Grandma for a lot of things that I didn’t know. I think deep down Dad had lots of thoughts never shared with anyone and he doesn’t want to put up with Grandma anymore. On top of that, I heard Dad’s been having rough nights at work, often not getting to bed until 3 AM, so exhaustion maybe another factor causing him lose control.

I have never seen Dad so angry, I think he needs anger management or at least watch that movie again. This time it wasn’t directed at me though, I was just a bystander. I think there is a long history between Dad and grandma and I heard it all today. I think Dad had buried all of his thoughts and tolerated grandma for so long until today that he finally exploded. I am not sure if Mom had anything to do with this, but it was scary to see Dad like this, he screamed and seemed totally lost control of himself during the arguments and it ended with Grandma hang up the phone. I will probably never hear from Grandma again, though I thought she was a nice lady.

The history between Dad and Grandma is a long one. Dad accused Grandma’s strong desire to try influence and control everyone around her. I do recall that Grandma would always tell Dad what she wants him to do, such as how to raise a baby, send me to learn piano, or wanting me to pick up ballerina, or learn how to swim…etc. She also constantly sends Dad these videos showing off other kid’s performing music. I don’t blame Dad. I am just a toddler and started learning how the world works around me and I got enough to learn already !

The other factor comes from Grandma’s nagging of asking Dad to get another baby. After what Mom’s been through and because of her age, I understand why they don’t want another baby, not that I can help, or is making things any better. But I think this is my Dad and Mom’s own decision to make. Not to mention that Grandma never helped taking care of me. I think she’s selfish wanting to fulfill her own desire.

Lastly, Grandma’s Republican political view is no help here either. I think maybe that’s what fundamentally shaped Grandma’s idealogy and all of these naggings. Grandma and step Grandpa love Donald Trump and they love themselves too. They think Trump will save the world, defeat the evil, and there is all corruptions in Democrats. Well, who’s the naive one now ? Dad always try to remind them to look at things from both sides, but they never listened, and eventually Dad and Grandma had to mutually agree to never bring up politics in their conversations. Dad thinks they are a “hopeless pair”, his words, not mine.

Alfred Adler suggested that all of our annoyance, troubles and depressions in life stem from relationships with others. The closer you are with someone, the more depression you can be and the more mood swings. I think he is right, maybe at one point Dad tried so hard to satisfy what Grandma wanted and forgot about himself. He’s an adult and should have his own will and should simply walk away from such complicated relationships. Alfred Adler also suggests never to blame today’s suffering on the past. Grandma does this a lot, she will blame how Chinese cultural revolution ruined her life, she blamed Great Grandma never gave her enough educations and that herself suffered and sacrificed a lot to get to where she is today. Nothing wrong with that, but living in the past is to live in the shadow.

There is no way to change the past, we can only change today, and future is just a combinations of moments that will occur because of decisions we are making today.

Living in other’s expectations and wanting others to behave the way you want them to be is also futile. In short, Alfred Adler’s idea is simply as simple as “mind your f* business”. How others judge your behavior, is other’s problem, not yours, and never try to behave in a way to avoid other’s judgement. Everyone judges differenlty and you can’t satisfy everyone.
